Bernhard Haslhofer is a faculty member and leader of the Financial Technologies research group at the Complexity Science Hub in Vienna. He also serves as an external lecturer at the Technical University of Vienna. His research interests lie in developing and applying data science methods to extract insights from large-scale and interconnected datasets.

Previously, Bernhard was a Senior Scientist and Thematic Coordinator for the Data Science & Artificial Intelligence research group at the Austrian Institute of Technology (AIT). Additionally, he has been an EU Marie Curie fellow at Cornell Information Science and an assistant professor at the University of Vienna. He holds a doctorate in Computer Science from the University of Vienna and a master's degree in Economics and Computer Science. 

Bernhard frequently collaborates in multidisciplinary settings and has published over 60 scientific articles in journals and conferences. He has organized various conferences, workshops, and symposia and has actively contributed to international standardization efforts with the National Information Standards Organization (NISO) and the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). Furthermore, he has coordinated several basic and applied research projects at both national and international levels.
