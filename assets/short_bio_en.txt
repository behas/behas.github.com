Bernhard Haslhofer is a research group leader at the Complexity Science Hub in Vienna. He previously held roles as a thematic coordinator in the Data Science & Artificial Intelligence research group at the Austrian Institute of Technology (AIT), a Marie Curie fellow at Cornell Information Science, and an assistant professor at the University of Vienna. He earned his doctorate in Computer Science from the University of Vienna and an M.S. in Economics and Computer Science from the Technical University of Vienna. His research focuses on developing and applying data science methods to extract insights from large-scale and interconnected datasets, with a particular emphasis on analyzing cryptoasset and decentralized finance ecosystems. A prolific collaborator in multidisciplinary settings, he has published over 60 scientific articles, contributed to international standardization efforts, and led numerous basic and applied research projects.
